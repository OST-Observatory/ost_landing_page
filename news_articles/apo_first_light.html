First Light APO

Our new PHOTOLINE 130 mm f/7 EDT Triplet Apo

We recently received a TS-Optics PHOTOLINE Apochromat. It has an aperture of 130mm and a focal length of 910mm, so the focal ratio is f/7. It is equipped with a triplet air gap lens with a SFPL-53-ED glass element from Japan and a 3.7" focuser. The focal ratio can be reduced to f5.2 with an optional reducer.

We plan to use it primarily for solar observations and deep sky imaging. We are also in the process of preparing some observations for the astrophysics lab courses. The large field of view of the APO allows us to do many new applications that are not possible with the CDK or Schmidt-Cassegrain telescopes. Compared to the CDK, the APO has almost 4 times the field of view. For example, we are planning to measure the star density of the Milky Way or to survey larger stellar populations for variable stars.

Durch glückliche Umstände haben wir noch eine weitere Neuanschaffung in der Pipeline… :-)
